Leicestershire

Swithland Res

female Lesser Scaup again on the south side from the causeway. There has been some confusion between the genuine bird (which doesn’t always seem to be present) and at least one female Tufted Duck with a similar white face patch. John Hague’s excellent comparison shot below, taken last Wednesday, shows the differences: 1) head shape – the Lesser Scaup has the crown rising to the classic smooth ‘bump’ on the rear crown. Tufted Ducks’ heads are always flatter on top, either with a short spiky tuft or sometimes nothing at all, but never a ‘bump’ like this. 2) the Lesser Scaup has bands and patches of pale grey along both mantle and flanks, but they aren’t plain grey – through a scope (and on all three photos on this page) you can clearly see distinct darker vermiculations in these patches, which are never shown by Tufted Ducks. 3) the bill, as well as being a bit bigger, is grey with black restricted to the nail. In dull light it can sometimes look a little darker around the sides of the nail, but it doesn’t have the broad dark tip that Tufted Ducks have. 4) the head is darker than most female Tufted Ducks, and in good light looks patchy, with some darker, almost purplish-brown feathers coming through. NB in addition to the white-faced female Tufted shown in John’s photo, there is at least one other bird present with even more white than this around the base of the bill. Fortunately the Lesser Scaup, when it’s there, is always quite close to the bridge, so the features should be visible through a scope at least, and often just through binoculars.
