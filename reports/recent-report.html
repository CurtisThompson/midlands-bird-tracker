Eyebrook Res

moulting adult drake Smew still – NB this bird has been reported as a first-winter, but first-winters don’t start getting adult head and body feathers until January at the earliest, and most will look similar to adult females until their first summer. The bird at EBR has extensive white coming through on the head and body, and also the characteristic adult male black loral patch, which again first-winters don’t get until well into the new year.
